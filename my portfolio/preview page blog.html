Using AI to Predict and Prevent Epidemics

AI is transforming how we detect and control disease outbreaks. By analyzing real-time data—from social media, hospital records, environmental sensors, and global travel—AI tools identify emerging threats before they spread globally.

Advanced machine learning models, including LLMs and graph neural networks, can forecast infection trajectories and pinpoint hotspots. Researchers from Johns Hopkins and Duke have recently shown that an AI-powered model named PandemicLLM accurately predicts COVID‑19 case trends up to three weeks ahead.

Network scientists at Northeastern are developing context-aware AI that factors in social systems and human mobility to model future epidemics better. With increased investment in health infrastructure and global data collaboration, AI-driven tools are becoming essential for early detection and targeted response strategies.
